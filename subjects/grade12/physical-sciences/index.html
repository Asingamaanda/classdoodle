Paper 1: Mechanics Essentials

Key Concepts

  • Displacement (Œîx): Change in position, vector (m).
  • Velocity (v): Rate of change of displacement, vector (m¬∑s‚Åª¬π).
  • Acceleration (a): Rate of change of velocity, vector (m¬∑s‚Åª¬≤).
  • Free‚Äëfall: Motion under gravity only, $a = g = 9.8\,\text{m¬∑s}^{-2}$ downward.

Equations of Motion (constant acceleration):

$v = u + at$

$\Delta x = ut + \tfrac{1}{2}at^2$

$v^2 = u^2 + 2a\Delta x$

Momentum & Impulse

Momentum: $p = mv$ (vector). Unit: kg¬∑m¬∑s‚Åª¬π.

Impulse: $J = F\Delta t = \Delta p$.

Conservation of Momentum: In an isolated system, total momentum before = total momentum after.

Exam Reminders

  • Define the system clearly before applying conservation.
  • Use sign convention (right positive, left negative) consistently.
  • Show momentum before and after separately.

Work, Energy & Power

Work: $W = F\Delta x\cos\theta$.

Kinetic Energy: $K = \tfrac{1}{2}mv^2$.

Gravitational Potential Energy: $U = mgh$.

Power: $P = \tfrac{W}{t}$.

Work‚ÄëEnergy Theorem

Net work done on an object equals the change in kinetic energy: $W_{net} = \Delta K$.

Doppler Effect (Sound)

Doppler Effect: Apparent change in frequency due to relative motion between source and observer.

Formula (sound): $f' = f\left(\tfrac{v \pm v_o}{v \mp v_s}\right)$

  • Use $+$ for motion towards, $-$ for motion away.
  • $v$ = speed of sound ($\approx 340\,\text{m¬∑s}^{-1}$ at $20^\circ$C).

Intermolecular Forces

Isomers

Geometric (cis‚Äëtrans): different arrangement around a double bond.
