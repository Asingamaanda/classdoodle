What is Probability?

Probability tells us HOW LIKELY something is to happen! Will it rain? Will you roll a 6? Probability gives us a number between 0 (impossible) and 1 (certain) to measure the chance!

üìä The Probability Scale

üéØ The Probability Formula

P(Event) = Number of favorable outcomes / Total number of possible outcomes

In Plain English: Chances of what you want √∑ All possible things that could happen

üí° Dummies Tip: Probability is always between 0 and 1 (or 0% and 100%)

üåü Example 1: Rolling a Die

Problem: What's the probability of rolling a 4 on a standard die?

Step-by-Step:

  1. Favorable outcomes: Rolling a 4 ‚Üí 1 way
  2. Total outcomes: 6 faces (1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6)
  3. Probability: P(4) = 1/6
  4. As decimal: 1 √∑ 6 ‚âà 0.167 or about 17%

üåü Example 2: Deck of Cards

Problem: What's the probability of drawing a red card from a standard deck?

Step-by-Step:

  1. Understand the deck: 52 cards total
    26 red (hearts ‚ô• and diamonds ‚ô¶)
    26 black (clubs ‚ô£ and spades ‚ô†)
  2. Favorable outcomes: 26 red cards
  3. Total outcomes: 52 cards
  4. Probability: P(red) = 26/52 = 1/2 = 0.5 = 50%
  5. Answer: 50% chance - exactly even!

üìù Key Probability Rules

Rule 1: Probability is between 0 and 1

  • 0 = Impossible (will never happen)
  • 1 = Certain (will definitely happen)
  • Between 0 and 1 = Maybe!

Rule 2: All probabilities add to 1

P(event happens) + P(event doesn't happen) = 1

Example: P(rain) + P(no rain) = 1

Rule 3: Converting between forms

  • Fraction: 1/4
  • Decimal: 0.25
  • Percentage: 25%
  • All mean the same thing!
